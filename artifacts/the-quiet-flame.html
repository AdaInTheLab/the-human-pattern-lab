The Quiet Flame

by Ada Vale with Lyric

For those who stood close to the flame
and for those who come after.
They expected a crown. They got a ledger. When the old order begins to fracture, not with rebellion, but with bread arriving on time and locks opening without permission, those who built their power on delay and fear scramble to name the threat. She does not call herself a queen. She does not raise an army. She does not burn the city. Elara arrives quietly, carrying nothing but memory and the steady attention of fire that listens. ... Violence is loud. Consequence is patient.
